Search within

Search within the title, abstract, claims, or full patent document: You can restrict your search to a specific field using field names.

Use TI= to search in the title, AB= for the abstract, CL= for the claims, or TAC= for all three. For example, TI=(safety belt).

Search by Cooperative Patent Classifications (CPCs): These are commonly used to represent ideas in place of keywords, and can also be entered in a search term box. If you're searching forseat belts, you could also search for B60R22/00 to retrieve documents that mention safety belts or body harnesses. CPC=B60R22 will match documents with exactly this CPC, CPC=B60R22/low matches documents with this CPC or a child classification of this CPC.

Find patents

Keywords and boolean syntax (USPTO or EPO format): seat belt searches these two words, or their plurals and close synonyms. "seat belt" searches this exact phrase, in order. -seat -belt searches for documents not containing either word.

For searches using boolean logic, the default operator is AND with left associativity. Note: this means safety OR seat belt is searched as (safety OR seat) AND belt. Each word automatically includes plurals and close synonyms. Adjacent words that are implicitly ANDed together, such as (safety belt), are treated as a phrase when generating synonyms.

Search by

Chemistry searches match terms (trade names, IUPAC names, etc. extracted from the entire document, and processed from .MOL files.)

Substructure (use SSS=) and similarity (use ~) searches are limited to one per search at the top-level AND condition. Exact searches can be used multiple times throughout the search query.

Searching by SMILES or InChi key requires no special syntax. To search by SMARTS, use SMARTS=.

To search for multiple molecules, select "Batch" in the "Type" menu. Enter multiple molecules separated by whitespace or by comma.

Where your project data is stored

Your project is stored locally on your computer inside the browser. Anyone using your browser/computer can access your project in the same way they have access to files stored locally stored on your computer.

This means that deleting cookies in the browser will delete your project data. Also note that browsers, by default, evict storage for websites when they are running low on disk space.

To prevent accidental loss of data, back up your project by exporting it to a file.

Safari users: After 7 days of inactivity, Safari deletes all browser storage.

How to share your project

You can share this project with others by exporting your project and having them import it on their own computer.

How to delete your data

You can delete data from your browser at any time by going to your project settings and clicking Delete Project.

Your Google account

By default, your project is not associated with your Google account, even if you are logged in. Logging in or out of your Google account has no effect on your project.
